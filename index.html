Philosophy

Strategy before pixels.
Outcomes before decoration.

Design is a problem-solving discipline, not surface treatment. Before opening Figma, I want to know what we're trying to change for the business and what we're trying to make easier for the people on the other side of the screen.

Business Growth

I design with conversion, retention, and revenue opportunities in mind — translating product goals into flows that help users succeed and help the business measure what's working.

Human-Centered Design

Every interface is a relationship. I design for empathy, accessibility, and trust — reducing cognitive load and emotional friction so people can move forward with confidence.

Product Clarity

Complex workflows, dense dashboards, and tangled navigation become understandable when information is structured around real decisions. Clarity is a feature.

Scalable Systems

Reusable components, documented patterns, and shared design language make collaboration with engineering smoother and ship cycles faster — without losing craft.

About

A designer who thinks like a product partner.

“
Good design isn't a layer applied at the end. It's the way a team understands the problem together — and the way users feel when they finally get what they came for.
— Alaa Reda

I started in web and UI design and grew into UX and product design over the past decade. That foundation in visual craft and front-end production still shapes how I work — I care about how a design feels at every pixel, and I know what's realistic to build.

Today I lead end-to-end product work at Coral Technology: from discovery and research, through information architecture, interaction design, and high-fidelity UI, to handoff and implementation review with engineering. I'm comfortable in the room with product, engineering, and business stakeholders, and I see translating between those perspectives as part of the job.

What I value most: research that respects users' time, decisions backed by clear rationale, and designs that hold up against real-world constraints. I believe design should serve both the people who use the product and the business that builds it — and that those two goals are far more aligned than they're sometimes made to sound.
