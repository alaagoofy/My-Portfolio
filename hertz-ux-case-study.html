The Brief

Hertz is one of the world's largest car rental brands. Despite its global reach, the mobile experience was falling behind — riddled with friction, unclear navigation, and a checkout flow that drove users away.

The Challenge
Redesign the Hertz mobile app to reduce booking drop-offs and deliver an experience worthy of a premium global brand.
My Approach
Applied the full Design Thinking framework — Empathize, Define, Ideate, Prototype, and Test — anchoring every decision in real user needs.

● Phase 01 · Empathize

Understanding the People Behind the Problem

Before touching a single wireframe, I needed to understand who rents cars, why, and what gets in their way. I conducted user interviews, ride-along observations, and competitive analysis.

01

Trust Anxiety at Checkout

Users reported feeling uncertain about hidden fees and insurance charges. 6 out of 8 interviewees abandoned the app at the payment screen.

02

Navigation Paralysis

The existing information architecture forced users through 7 steps to complete a booking. Participants lost track of where they were in the flow.

03

Car Discovery Confusion

Users couldn't distinguish between vehicle categories. Unclear imagery and jargon-heavy specs made car selection feel like a gamble.

04

No Sense of Progress

Absence of booking status indicators created anxiety. Users didn't know if their reservation was confirmed until they checked email.

05

Loyalty Program Invisibility

Frequent renters were unaware of their Hertz Gold Plus Rewards status within the app. A key retention lever was effectively hidden.

06

Poor Offline Handling

Travelers in airports — prime rental moments — experienced app failures in low-connectivity zones, forcing them to the website or counter.

● Phase 03 · Ideate

Generating Solutions Without Judgment

Armed with clear problem statements, I ran rapid ideation sessions — sketching over 40 concepts across three key interaction areas before converging on the most promising directions.

Radical Simplification of the Booking Flow

Collapsed 7 booking steps into 3 focused screens: Search → Select → Confirm. Each screen has one clear job. Progress is communicated via a persistent top bar.

Visual-First Car Cards

Replaced text-heavy category lists with large, photographic car cards featuring a prominent price, key specs (seats, transmission, fuel type), and a clear rental class name in plain language.

Transparent Pricing Component

Designed a "Total Cost Breakdown" module visible from the car detail screen — before the user ever reaches payment. Every line item is labeled in plain language.

Loyalty-Aware Personalization

The home screen dynamically surfaces Gold Plus Rewards status, points balance, and a one-tap "re-book your last rental" shortcut for returning customers.

Animated Confirmation Screen

A celebratory in-app confirmation with booking reference, pick-up map pin, and a "Add to Wallet" shortcut — replacing the previous redirect to email.

● Phase 04 · Prototype

The Screens

High-fidelity screens built in Adobe XD, preserving Hertz's iconic yellow identity while establishing a clean, confidence-inspiring design language.


Design Rationale

Key Design Decisions

Every UI decision traces back to a specific user need or pain point identified in research.

🟡

Hertz Yellow as a Trust Signal

Rather than diluting the brand's iconic yellow, I leaned into it — using it for primary CTAs and confirmations to associate Hertz's color with positive actions and completion states.

📊

3-Step Progress Bar

Collapsed the 11-step flow into 3 macro-stages (Search, Confirm, Pay). The persistent progress bar eliminates "where am I?" anxiety and sets accurate expectations.

💰

Upfront Price Transparency

Total cost — including taxes, GPS, and any add-ons — is shown on the car detail screen before the user ever initiates checkout. This removed the most common drop-off trigger.

🎴

Visual Car Cards with Plain Language

Replaced category codes (CDW, SDAR) with plain English descriptors. Specs are rendered as icon + value pairs, scannable at a glance without needing to read prose.

⭐

Loyalty-First Home Screen

Returning Gold members see their points balance and status above the fold. This acknowledges their relationship with Hertz and creates an immediate reason to stay in the app.

🎊

Celebratory Confirmation

The confirmation screen is designed to feel like an achievement, not a receipt. Booking reference, car details, and wallet integration are all available instantly — no email required.

06 · Reflection

What I Learned & What's Next

Every project teaches. Here's an honest look at what went well, what I'd do differently, and where this design could go.

🟢 Lessons Learned

  • Transparency is a design feature — showing the full price early built more trust than any visual treatment could.
  • Design thinking loops back. The "define" phase evolved twice as testing revealed new pain points around add-on selection.
  • Constraints from brand guidelines (Hertz yellow) can be creative constraints — not limitations.
  • A simple progress bar does more for perceived usability than any amount of visual polish.
  • Testing with real frequent renters revealed use cases I never would have imagined from a desk.

→ Next Steps

  • Design and test the vehicle damage inspection flow — a major post-pickup pain point not addressed in v1.
  • Explore a map-first discovery experience for users who browse by airport or city area.
  • Design the Hertz Gold member "fast lane" experience — reducing booking to a single confirmable card.
  • Prototype offline mode showing cached booking details for airport travelers with limited connectivity.
  • Conduct A/B test on the price transparency component to measure conversion lift vs. control.
